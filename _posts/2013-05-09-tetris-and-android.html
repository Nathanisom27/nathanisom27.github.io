The other day, I was really craving android development, mostly due to the fact that it was something I half-assed started this semester. Then this glorious cheat-sheet start guide for creating an app with eclipse appeared and I had a new project to work on. I think for an initial project on Android it would be good to go with Tetris, as it something that has been done many times before, and I am certain common piece algorithms exist.

Hm. Going to go look that up now.

Alright, so according to this, Tetris pieces are generated in 3 different fashions:

  • Completely Random
  • "Fairly" Random - assuring an equal amount of pieces get distributed over time.
  • "Bastet" generation - which is where the piece most likely to do harm to the player is picked.

For my implementation, it would probably be easiest to simply be completely random with the pieces, so that is what I will do at first. Later, as a challenge, I will add an option for Bastet Tetris.
