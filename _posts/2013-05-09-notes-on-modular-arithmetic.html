Looking at equations of the type:

ax+b=c(mod n) where {a,b,c} are numbers, x is unknown, and equation is mod n.

Example from book:

3x+2=4(mod 5)

3x+2+3=4+3(mod 5) to eliminate 2, by turning it into a 5, because 5(mod 5)=0

3x = 2(mod 5)

2*3x=2*2(mod 5)

6x = 4 (mod 5)

x = 4(mod 5)

So, one variable sided modular arithmetic isn't that bad, but the current problem I have is with two x's on either side:

4x+7=x+2(mod 10)

Oh balls. Okay, so I was just intimidated by the fact the mod was in the equation. You can simply subtract an x from each side and solve from there. :D
